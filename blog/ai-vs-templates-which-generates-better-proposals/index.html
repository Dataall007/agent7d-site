AI vs templates: which generates better proposals?

📅 July 14, 2026 ✍️ agent7d Team ⏱ 5 min read

The Problem

Most teams that use monday.com for project management or sales pipelines eventually hit the same wall: everything they need to write a proposal is already in the system — the client name, the scope, the budget, the timeline — but getting that information into an actual document is still a manual, error-prone process.

The typical workaround is a template. You build a Word doc or Google Doc with placeholder text, then spend 20 minutes copying fields over, reformatting things that broke when you pasted them, and hoping you didn't miss a detail. Multiply that by ten proposals a month and you've lost a full workday to a task that should take minutes.

Templates solve the blank-page problem. They don't solve the copy-paste problem. And they definitely don't solve the "this proposal reads like a generic form letter" problem.

The Solution

AI-generated documents — specifically ones that pull live data from your monday.com items — close all three gaps at once.

Here's the key distinction: a static template is a structure waiting to be filled. An AI-generated document is a structure that fills itself and adapts its language based on the context of that specific deal, project, or client.

When you generate a proposal from a monday.com item, the AI isn't just swapping {{client_name}} for "Acme Corp." It's reading the scope column, the budget column, the notes your sales rep added, and it's writing contextually appropriate sentences around that data. The result reads like someone actually wrote it for that client — because, in a meaningful sense, something did.

For teams sending five to fifty proposals a month, this isn't a nice-to-have. It's the difference between a process that scales and one that breaks the moment you're busy.

Step by Step

Here's how a typical AI-powered proposal workflow looks when connected to monday.com:

1. Structure your monday.com board correctly. Make sure each item captures the data you'd want in a proposal: client name, project scope, deliverables, timeline, budget, and any custom notes. The cleaner your board, the better the output.

2. Select the item you want to generate a proposal for. This could be a new lead in your CRM board, a project kick-off item, or a renewal opportunity.

3. Choose your document type. Proposal, contract, statement of work — depending on the tool, you select what you need and the AI knows which structure and tone to apply.

4. Review and adjust. AI-generated doesn't mean fire-and-forget. Give it a 60-second read. You'll typically find the document is 90% ready and needs a minor tweak, not a rewrite.

5. Send or export. Download as PDF, copy to your preferred tool, or send directly from the platform.

Total time from item to polished proposal: under three minutes once you're set up.

Real Example

A digital agency using monday.com to manage client onboarding used to spend about 45 minutes putting together each project proposal. Their board had everything — service tier, deliverables, estimated hours, pricing — but the document still had to be assembled by hand.

After switching to AI-generated proposals pulled directly from monday.com items, their average proposal time dropped to under five minutes. More importantly, the quality became more consistent. Junior team members could generate the same quality proposal as senior ones, because the AI was working from the same structured data every time.

They also noticed something unexpected: win rates improved slightly. The proposals felt more tailored, even though they were being produced faster. Clients noticed the specificity.

How to Get Started

If you're already using monday.com and your board has reasonably clean data, you're closer than you think to making this work.

Start by auditing one board — your sales pipeline or project intake is usually the best candidate. Ask yourself: if someone read only this item, would they have everything needed to write a proposal? If the answer is yes, you're ready.

If not, add two or three columns. A scope summary field, a key deliverables column, and a notes section will take you from zero to generatable in an afternoon.

Once your data is clean, the generation part is fast. The bigger shift is cultural: getting your team to trust that a three-minute AI proposal can be better than a 45-minute manual one. It can. The data backs it up.
