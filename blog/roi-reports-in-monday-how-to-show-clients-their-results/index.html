ROI reports in monday — how to show clients their results

📅 July 18, 2026 ✍️ agent7d Team ⏱ 5 min read

ROI Reports in Monday — How to Show Clients Their Results

You track everything in monday.com. Hours logged, tasks completed, budgets spent, milestones hit. The data is all there. But when it's time to show a client what they actually got for their money, most teams still spend hours manually pulling numbers into a Google Doc or PowerPoint deck.

There's a better way to do this — and once you set it up, client reporting becomes something you look forward to instead of dread.

---

The Problem

monday.com is excellent at capturing operational data, but it wasn't designed to produce polished, client-facing documents. The typical workaround looks like this: export to CSV, open a spreadsheet, calculate totals, copy numbers into a template, format everything, add your logo, double-check the math, and finally send it — only to realize you pulled last week's data by mistake.

Beyond the time cost, there's a consistency problem. Different team members produce different-looking reports. Clients notice. It signals disorganization even when the actual work was excellent.

The real issue is that your monday.com boards already contain everything a strong ROI report needs. You're just not extracting it efficiently.

---

The Solution

Build your ROI reporting workflow directly inside monday.com, then automate the document generation step.

The approach has two parts. First, structure your boards so that ROI-relevant data is clean, calculated, and in one place. Second, use a tool that reads that data and generates a formatted, professional report without you touching a single template.

This turns a two-hour manual process into something that takes about 30 seconds.

---

Step by Step

1. Standardize your board columns for reporting

Every client project board should have a consistent set of columns: budget allocated, actual spend, hours estimated, hours logged, deliverables planned, deliverables completed, and a formula column that calculates budget variance or ROI percentage. If you're using monday's Formula column, you can calculate these automatically.

2. Add a summary group

Create a group at the top of each board called "Client Summary" or "Reporting View." Pull key metrics here using mirror columns or summary rows. This becomes the data source your report will pull from.

3. Use status columns to track deliverable completion

Instead of writing notes about what got done, use Status columns with clear labels (Delivered, In Progress, Planned). These give you clean, count-ready data that translates directly into report language — "14 of 16 deliverables completed" is more compelling than a paragraph of explanation.

4. Generate the report from the item data

This is where DocuAI comes in. Once your board is structured, you point DocuAI at the relevant item or group, select your ROI report template, and it generates a formatted document using your actual board data. No copying. No formatting. The numbers are current because they come directly from monday.

5. Review and send

Give it a 60-second read, add any qualitative context you want to include, and send. The report looks professional because it's built from a template you control, populated with live data.

---

Real Example

A digital marketing agency manages 12 client accounts in monday.com. Each board tracks ad spend, campaign deliverables, hours, and results by channel.

Previously, monthly reporting took their account managers about 90 minutes per client — 18 hours total across the team. The reports also looked inconsistent because each person had their own version of the template.

After restructuring their boards to standardize the reporting columns and connecting DocuAI to their board, each report now takes under five minutes. The template is locked in. The data pulls automatically. Account managers spend the time they saved actually analyzing results and adding strategic commentary instead of formatting spreadsheets.

Client satisfaction scores went up. Not because the results changed — because the reporting finally reflected the quality of the work.

---

How to Get Started

Start with one client board. Add or clean up the columns mentioned above — budget, spend, hours, deliverables. Create a summary group. Make sure your formula columns are calculating correctly.

Then think about what your ideal ROI report actually looks like. What sections does it need? What numbers matter most to that client? Build that structure once, and you have a reusable template.

The goal is to make reporting a byproduct of the work you're already tracking, not a separate project that eats your Fridays.
