5 documents every sales team should automate in monday

📅 July 9, 2026 ✍️ agent7d Team ⏱ 5 min read

5 Documents Every Sales Team Should Automate in Monday

The Problem

Your sales team is spending a meaningful chunk of every deal cycle doing something a junior intern could do: copying information from monday.com into Word documents.

Deal name from the board → paste into proposal. Contact name → paste into contract. Pricing tier → paste into quote. Close date → paste into agreement. Repeat fifty times a week, across every rep, for every deal at every stage.

This is not a small inefficiency. It is a systematic drain on the people you hired to sell. Every minute a rep spends reformatting a document is a minute they are not on the phone, not following up, not closing. And beyond the time cost, manual data entry creates errors — wrong pricing, wrong client names, outdated terms — that can stall deals or create legal headaches after the fact.

The root cause is simple: your CRM data lives in monday.com, but your documents live somewhere else, disconnected.

The Solution

The fix is document automation directly inside monday.com. Instead of exporting data and reformatting it manually, you build templates once and let the system generate the finished document from the item data that already exists on your board.

No copy-pasting. No version confusion. No "I used last month's template by accident."

The five documents below are where sales teams get the most immediate ROI from automation. If you start anywhere, start here.

Step by Step

1. Sales Proposals

Your proposal template contains fields that change per deal: client name, company, proposed solution, pricing, rep name, valid-through date. All of that data already exists as columns in your monday.com board. Map those columns to your template once, and every proposal generates in seconds from any item.

2. NDAs and Confidentiality Agreements

NDAs are high-frequency and low-creativity. The only thing that changes is the counterparty's information. Automating these frees up legal review time for agreements that actually need it, and gets NDAs out the door same-day instead of waiting in someone's drafts folder.

3. Sales Contracts and Order Forms

Contract generation is where errors are most expensive. When pricing, payment terms, and scope are pulled directly from approved fields in monday.com, you eliminate the risk of a rep accidentally quoting last quarter's pricing or mistyping a contract value.

4. Follow-Up and Proposal Summary Emails

Not every document is a formal PDF. Structured follow-up emails — recapping the call, summarizing the proposed solution, listing next steps — can be templated and generated from your board just as easily. Reps personalize the tone; the substance populates automatically.

5. Pipeline and Deal Status Reports

Weekly pipeline reports for leadership are another repetitive task that automation handles cleanly. Pull deal stage, projected close, deal value, and rep name from your board into a formatted report template. What used to take 45 minutes on a Friday afternoon takes 30 seconds.

Real Example

A six-person SaaS sales team was generating proposals manually using a shared Google Doc template. Reps would duplicate it, fill in client details, adjust pricing, update the date, and send — a process that took 20-30 minutes per proposal and produced inconsistent formatting.

They mapped their monday.com board columns to a DocuAI proposal template: company name, contact, subscription tier, monthly price, contract length, and rep signature block. After setup, generating a proposal from any deal item took under a minute. The documents were consistent, branded, and accurate every time.

In the first month, they calculated roughly 8 hours of reclaimed selling time per rep. More importantly, proposals started going out same-day instead of next-day, which their reps credited with improving their response rate on competitive deals.

How to Get Started

If you want to replicate this, the process is straightforward:

  • Identify which of the five document types above causes the most friction for your team right now.
  • Audit your monday.com board to confirm the relevant data columns exist and are being filled in consistently.
  • Build or upload your document template, mapping each variable to the corresponding column.
  • Test the output on three or four real deals before rolling it out to the full team.
  • Add document generation as a step in your deal stage automation — so it triggers at the right moment without anyone having to remember.

    • Start with one document type. Get it working well. Then expand.
